Bistek

This recipe utilized meats like pork, calamansi extract and soy sauce with spices like pepper to create an amazing dish.


Unlike the other variation, this recipe utilized the lime extract along with the calamansi extract
to further enchance its flavors.

unlike the other recipes of bistek which its taste is very generic
and plain boring. The pork used in my recipe was placed inside the

Ingredient List:

  • Pork meat
  • Lime extract
  • Calamansi extract
  • Soy sauce
  • Pepper
  • Onion
  • Salt
  • Sugar
  • Oil
  • Water

Cooking Instructions:

  1. Marinate the pork chops – First, combine 4 pieces of pork chops with 5 tablespoons of soy sauce, 2 tablespoons
    of sugar, and the juice from 2 pieces of lime or calamansi in a large bowl or resealable bag. The soy sauce adds
    a salty and umami flavor, while the lime juice provides acidity that balances the richness of the pork. Marinate
    the pork for at least 1 hour; for best results, consider marinating it overnight. This allows the flavors to deeply
    penetrate the meat, resulting in a more flavorful and tender pork chop steak.
  2. Sear the pork chops – Next, heat a pan and add ½ cup of cooking oil. Once the oil is hot and shimmering, carefully
    place the marinated pork chops into the pan. Pan-fry the pork over medium heat for about 3 minutes on each side. This
    cooking method creates a lovely golden-brown crust while locking in moisture. Be cautious not to overcrowd the pan; cook
    in batches if necessary to ensure even cooking and browning.
  3. Simmer until tender – After frying, remove any excess oil from the pan to prevent the dish from becoming greasy. Pour in
    the remaining marinade along with 1 ½ cups of water, then bring the mixture to a rolling boil. Reduce the heat and let it
    simmer for about 45 minutes. This slow cooking allows the pork to become tender and absorb the flavors of the sauce. Check
    occasionally and add more water if needed to keep the pork chops submerged and prevent drying out.
  4. Season and add the onions – Once the pork is tender, add ½ teaspoon of granulated white sugar, salt, and pepper to taste. Stir
    well to incorporate these seasonings, which balance the dish’s flavors. Then, introduce 2 sliced white onions into the pan and
    cook for an additional 3 minutes. The onions will soften and caramelize slightly, enhancing the sauce’s richness and adding a
    layer of sweetness to your pork chop steak.
  5. Serve with rice – Finally, turn off the heat and carefully transfer the pork chops to a serving plate, ensuring to drizzle some
    of the sauce over the top for added flavor. Garnish with the cooked onions for an appealing presentation. Serve and enjoy the
    savory flavors of your homemade pork chop steak!
